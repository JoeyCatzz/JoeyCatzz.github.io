Total Cash from Operating Activities

This is the amount of cash that is made from a business main operations. example: Volvo makes money with the production and sale of car's.
This section starts with the net income and adds back the depreciation and amortization. Though they are real expenses, they don't eat up any cash, because they represent cash that was eaten up years ago.

Total Cash from Investing Activities

This is the sum of capital expanditures and other investing cash flow items.

Capital Expenditures (CAPEX)

Money spend by a business on acquiring or maintaining fixed assets, such as land, buildings, and equipment.
These are assets that are expensed over a period of time greater than a year through depreciation or amortiszation.
They also include expenditures for such intangibles as patents.

Other Investing Cash Flow Items

This is the cash that gets expended and brought in from buying and selling of income-producing assets.

Total Cash from Financing Activities

This measures the cash that flows in and out of a company because of financing activities. This includes all cash for the payment of dividends. It also includes the selling and buying of the company's stocks and bonds.

Cash dividends paid and share buybacks

Issuance (Retirement) of Stock, Net = share buybacks
When a company payes dividends to the shareholder. The shareholder must pay income tax on the dividends.
It is more intresting for the company to buy back shares.

Net Change in Cash

When we add the cash from operating activities, investing activities and financing activities we get the Net Chage in Cash.

Free Cash Flow

Total Cash from Operating Activities - Capital Expenditures(CAPEX) = Free Cash Flow
This is how much cash is left for the shareholders!
There are 5 things the company can do with it's free cash flow:

  • expand operations
  • acquisitions
  • reduce costs
  • pay dividents
  • buy back shares
This is called capital allocation.
