Cash Flow Statement

Cash flow statements are like income statements in that they always cover a set period of time.
Companies use the Accrual Method of accounting, this means that sales are booked when the goods go out the door. Even if the buyer takes years to pay them.
Because almost all business extend some kind of credit to their buyers. The Accrual Method allows them to book the sales on credit as income under accounts receivable on the income statement.
To track the of the cash that flows in and out of the business you have the Cash Flow Statement.
The Cash Flow Statement will tell us if the company has a positive or nagative cash flow.
The Cash Flow Statement breaks down into three sections: operating activities, investing activities and financing activities.

Total Cash from Operating Activities

Net Income
+ Depreciation
+ Amortization
= Total Cash from Operating Activities

Total Cash from Investing Activities

- Capital Expenditures
+ Other Investing Cash Flow Items
= Total Cash from Investing Activities

Total Cash from Financing Activities

Cash Dividends Paid
Issuance (Retirement) of Stock, Net
Issuance (Retirement) of Debt, Net
= Total Cash from Financing Activities

Net Change in Cash

Total Cash from Operating Activities
+ Total Cash from Investing Activities
+ Total Cash from Financing Activities
= Net Change in Cash
