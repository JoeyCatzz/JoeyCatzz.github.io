Income Statement

Income statements tell the investor the result of the company's operations for a set period of time.
An income statement has 3 components: First, there is the revenu of the business. Then there is the firm's expenses which are subtracted from the revenue and tell us wheter the company earned a profit or had a loss.

Revenue
- Cost of Goods Sold
= Gross Profit

Operating Expenses
- Selling, General & Admin.
- Research & Development
- Depreciation
= Operating Profit

- Interest Expense
+ (-) Gain (Loss) Sale Assets
- Other
= Income Before Tax

- Income Taxes Paid
= Net Earnings
