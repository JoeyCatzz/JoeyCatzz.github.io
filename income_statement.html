Revenue

The first line on the income statement is always total, or gross, revenue.
The fact that a company has a lot of revenue doesn't mean that it is earning a profit.
Total revenue minus expenses eaquals net earnings.

Gross Profit

Gross profit is how much money the company made off of total revenue after subtracting the costs of the raw goods and the labor used to make the goods.
It doesn't include such categories as sales and administrative costs, depreciation, and the intersest cost of running the business.

Gross profit/Total Revenues * 100 = Gross Profit Margin
Companies with a Gross Profit margin of 40% or better tend to be companies with some sort of durable competitive advantage.

Net Earnings

net earnings/net income/bottom line
Where we find out how much money the company has made after it paid income taxes.
Are the net earnings showing an historical upward trend? A single years entry for net earnings is useless.
The ride does not have to be smooth but there must be an upward historical trend.
