Balance Sheet

Balance sheets are only for a set date. We can create a balance sheet for any day of the year.
A company's accounting department will generate a balance sheet at the end of each financial quarter.
The balance sheet shows us how much the company has in assets (cash, property, ...) and how much money it owes to vendors, the banks, and the bondholders.
A balance sheet is broken into two parts: all the assets and second the the liabilities and shareholder equity.
Under liabilities we find two categories: Current Liabilities (money that is owed within the year) and Long-Term Liabilities (liabilities that come due in one year or more).
Assets - Liabilities = net worth of the business, which is the same as Shareholders' equity.
