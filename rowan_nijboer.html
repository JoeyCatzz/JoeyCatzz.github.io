The System

To value a company we must look at the earnings model, the competitive advantage, Managment and the Financials.

The earnings model

How does the company makes money? Where does the company makes money?
Stay in your circle of competence.

Competitive advantage

Does the company have a moat?

Low cost production:
Is it easy to start another company that does the excact same thing or is it to expesive? example: apple
Can the company can produce cheaper product than the compition? example: basic-fit.

High Switching cost:
Can a consumer easily swith to another company? example: busnisses running on windows

Network effects:
The more customers the business has the better it gets. example: Waze

Intangible assets:
Does the company have a strong brand: example Coca-Cola

Efficien Scale:
Is the company scalable? example UPS

Managment

Who is managing the company? Managment must be focused on the long term strategy.
CEO,CFO, COO. Do these people have integrity?
Is it a founder led company?
Skin in the game? Do they own shares? As a rule of thump the CEO should own 5 times his year salary in shares.

Financials

Is the company making any money?

  1. A thing Rowan looks out for is the EBITA. Rowan is not a fan of a company using EBITA to talk about it's profit. He is more instrested in the net earnings of a company.
  2. A second thing Rowan is intrested in is the Free Cash Flow. Stay away if the free cash flow is negative. This means the company is not able to make money with its business.
    What should the company do with it's free cash flow?
    • Acquisitions are in most cases not good.
    • Paying back debt is a good idea.
    • Dividents are something older companies do. (They don't grow anymore)
    • Share buybacks are good. If the share price is lower than the intrinsic value.

Finantial Ratios

  1. Debt Position: Debt to EBITA Ratio must be smaller than 3.
  2. Net earnings show great consistency (upward trend) over the last 10 years.

External Factors
